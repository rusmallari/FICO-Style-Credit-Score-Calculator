How to fill this out
01
Credit cards
One card is added by default. If you have more, click + Add credit card for each one. For every card you'll need three things: how many payments you made on time in the past 12 months, how many were due in total (usually 12), and the card's credit limit and current balance. If you never missed a payment, on-time and total will be the same number.
02
Car loan
Only add this if you currently have an active car loan. Click + Car loan and enter how many payments you made on time and how many were due in the past 12 months. Most car loans are monthly, so total is usually 12. If your loan was paid off or you don't have one, skip this.
03
Student loan
Only add this if your student loans are currently in repayment. Click + Student loan and enter on-time payments and total payments due in the past 12 months. If your loans are still in deferment or grace period, you likely had no scheduled payments — skip it.
04
Mortgage
If you have a home loan, click + Mortgage and enter your on-time and total payments for the past 12 months. Like other installment loans, mortgages help your credit mix and contribute to payment history but don't affect your utilization ratio.
05
Personal loan
If you have a personal or other installment loan, click + Personal loan and enter on-time and total scheduled payments for the past 12 months. Only include loans that are currently active and in repayment.
06
Credit history length
Enter the average age of all your open accounts in years. To estimate: add up the age of each account and divide by the number of accounts. For example, a 6-year card, a 4-year card, and a 2-year loan = (6+4+2) ÷ 3 = 4 years. Tip: closing old accounts shortens this number, so it's usually better to keep them open even if unused.
07
Hard inquiries
Enter how many times a lender pulled your credit in the past 12 months — this happens when you apply for a new card, loan, or mortgage. Checking your own score doesn't count. If you shopped around for a single loan and multiple lenders checked your credit within a short window, that usually counts as just one inquiry.
08
Most recent late payment
Select how long ago your last missed payment was. A late payment from six months ago hurts your score much more than one from four years ago. If you have never missed a payment on any account ever, select "Never." This is one of the most important fields for accuracy.
09
Derogatory marks
Select the worst negative item currently on your credit report. A collection or charge-off (when a lender writes off your unpaid debt) can drop a good score by 50–110 points. A bankruptcy can drop it 130–240 points and stays on your report for 7–10 years. If you have none of these, select "None."
Common questions
Your credit score is one of the most influential numbers in your financial life. Lenders use it to decide whether to approve you for credit cards, car loans, mortgages, and more — and at what interest rate. A higher score means lower rates, which can save you thousands over the life of a loan. Landlords, insurers, and even some employers check it too.
A strong score unlocks better financial options — lower APRs, easier apartment approvals, cheaper car insurance, and larger loan amounts. A weak score can mean higher deposits, loan denials, or paying significantly more interest over time. Even a 50-point difference can change the mortgage rate you're offered by half a percent or more.
The two highest-impact moves are paying every bill on time and reducing credit utilization below 30% (ideally below 10%). Together these drive 65% of your score. Setting up autopay for at least the minimum eliminates late payments entirely. Paying down a high card balance can raise your score within a single billing cycle.
Utilization is the percentage of your available revolving credit you're currently using. If you have a $10,000 limit and carry a $3,000 balance, your utilization is 30%. Lenders see high utilization as financial strain. Keeping it under 30% is good; under 10% is excellent. It's one of the fastest factors to improve — paying down a balance helps immediately.
